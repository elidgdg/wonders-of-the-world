The Colosseum

The Colosseum in Rome was built in the first century by order of the Emperor Vespasian.

A feat of engineering, the ampitheater measures 620 by 513 feet and features a complex system of vaults. It was capable of holding 50,000 spectators, who watches a variety of events. Perhaps most notable were gladiator fights, though men battling animals was also common.
